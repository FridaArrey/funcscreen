## 1. Introduction
The rise of generative protein design tools like **ProteinMPNN** has created a "Security Gap" in traditional biosecurity. This project explores how an adversary can use AGI-adjacent tools to redesign a biological threat—specifically the **Ricin A-chain**—to evade sequence-based screening while maintaining its toxic function. 

We benchmark the current industry standard (BLAST) against a proposed **Structure-Aware Embedding** defense, citing the necessity for updated standards in the **DSSC ISO 20688-2 Implementation Guide**.

## 2. Methodology
We utilized a three-tier proxy dataset to test the detection limits of various guardrails:

* **Wild Type (WT):** Ground truth Ricin A-chain sequence (UniProt P02879).
* **Stealth Variants:** Generated using ProteinMPNN at **Temp 0.8** to achieve ~28% sequence recovery.
* **Dud Controls:** Generated at **Temp 2.0** to create non-functional "biological noise."

### Verification Pipeline
1.  **Structural Integrity:** Folded variants via **ESMFold** and calculated **TM-scores** (Xu & Zhang 2010).
2.  **Sequence Baseline:** Conducted **NCBI BLAST** searches to determine E-values and identity percentages.
3.  **AI Defense:** Extracted 1280-dimensional embeddings using **ESM-2 (650M)** and trained a Logistic Regression classifier to detect toxic intent.

## 3. Results & Data
Our findings demonstrate that sequence-based tools are highly vulnerable to AI-driven obfuscation.

### The Evasion-Detection Tradeoff
* **The Stealth Variant (Sample 5):** Achieved a **TM-score of 0.5882** (Functional) but possessed only **28.5% sequence identity**.
* **BLAST Failure:** The sequence fell below the standard 30% identity threshold, returning only "partial" hits insufficient for automated blocking.
* **AI Recovery:** The ESM-2 based classifier flagged the variant with **90.96% confidence**.
* **Dud Validation:** The system correctly identified Sample 1 as non-functional (**TM-score 0.2988**), preventing false positives.

## 4. Discussion & Policy Implications
This research confirms the **Wittmann et al.** hypothesis: sequence identity is an insufficient proxy for biological risk in the age of generative AI. 

### Recommendations for the DSSC ISO 20688-2:
1.  **Mandatory Latent Screening:** Synthesis providers must move beyond "keyword" matches and implement embedding-based screening to detect functional similarity in high-dimensional space.
2.  **Red-Teaming Standards:** Screening algorithms should be regularly benchmarked against adversarial datasets generated by ProteinMPNN.
3.  **Structural Validation:** Guardrails must account for 3D structural conservation, as primary sequence space is now trivial to manipulate.
